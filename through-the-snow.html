❈ CHAPTER ONE ❈

The traveler arrives

where the snow never stops, and every window glows warm.

Welcome, traveler.

"Through the Snow" is an interactive story I built in 2023 — my first real Unity project. A traveler enters an unfamiliar snow world to experience uncertainty, excitement, and growth.

The title works on two levels: it's both playfully exploring exciting new terrain, and persevering through hardship to push forward.

I wanted the player to feel like they'd just stepped inside the warmest inn on the coldest night. Every UI element is hand-painted to feel like it belongs in a storybook, not a menu.

❈ CHAPTER THREE ❈

Choose your cloak

before the traveler sets out, they pick the color they'll wear into the snow.

"Who will you be?"

I wanted the player to identify with their traveler, not just control them. So before the journey starts, you open an old oak wardrobe and pick a color — red, yellow, blue, purple, or green — and watch your character change in the soft glow of the lantern.

The wardrobe scene took the longest to paint. I wanted it to feel like an actual room, with the lantern casting light on one side and blue moonlight creeping in the other.

❈ ❈ ❈

Through the Snow was my very first Unity project — I built it solo in 2023 as a school assignment that I let get out of hand, because I loved the world too much to stop at the brief.

Every illustration in this page — the village, the inn, the wardrobe, the innkeeper, the traveler in five colors, the scrolls and lanterns — I painted by hand in Procreate. Every line of C# is mine. I wanted the game to feel like someone had drawn it in the margins of a very old storybook, and then somehow it learned how to respond when you clicked.

Illustration & art direction

All backgrounds, characters, UI scrolls, and props — painted in Procreate.

Unity + C# scripting

Scene flow, input fields, persistent player data, async scene loads.

UI design

Parchment menus, lantern accents, diegetic wardrobe, hand-lettered buttons.

Character design

01 — Inspiration
The sense no one talks about
Interoception is how your body knows it exists — heartbeat, breath, tension. When it breaks down, the world stops feeling real.

⚓ The problem

Someone close to our team experiences derealization — that deeply unsettling feeling that the world isn't real, that you've disconnected from your own body and surroundings.

Every existing grounding tool treats this as a cognitive problem, offering instructions to follow at the exact moment the thinking mind is the least reliable thing available.

🌊 Our approach

Instead of asking users to think their way back to their body, Anchor gives the interoceptive system direct, sensory inputs to work with.

When a dissociative shift is detected, the world simply becomes more alive. Bioluminescent fish appear on nearby surfaces. A soft ambient tone begins. No alarms, no clinical language — just an ocean that appears when you need it most.

02 — The Experience
Three ways to come back
Each exercise targets a different interoceptive channel — vision, touch, and breath — disguised as an immersive underwater world.
01
🐚

Collect Seashells

AR shells appear on surfaces around you. Reaching out and picking them up re-engages spatial awareness and tactile body sense through physical movement.

02
🐠

Snap the Fish

Bioluminescent fish swim through your environment. Tracking and photographing them reactivates visual seeking and the eye-brain connection that grounds you in space.

03
🫧

Breathe Underwater

The world slowly submerges. Guided breathing exercises restore the breath-heartbeat feedback loop, the most fundamental interoceptive signal.

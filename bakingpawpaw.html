Baking PawPaw

what's in your fridge today? ask Mr. Paw — he'll whisk up a recipe just for you.

A warm, hand-illustrated cooking game where your fridge becomes a recipe book. Pick your ingredients, chat with a tiny chef cat powered by Google's Gemini AI, and bake something cozy together. Built in Unity across seven interconnected scenes.

recipe card · 01

a walk through the cottage

seven Unity scenes, all lovingly illustrated, all wired together with singletons and prefab spawners

1

the main menu

A cozy opening scene set in Mr. Paw's kitchen — a painted room with a teal fridge, polaroids pinned to the wall, achievement badges on the shelf, a little TV playing a baking montage, and that beloved cat-paw-fish wallpaper. The scene gets loaded by StartButtonController.cs which handles the first SceneManager.LoadSceneAsync call.

● StartButtonController.cs ● Scene 0 → Scene 1
2

the fridge scene

Click the fridge to swing it open and find 10 wet ingredients illustrated by hand — eggs, milk, cream, butter, oil, yeast, vanilla, cream cheese, flour, chocolate. Each is a Unity prefab with its own spawn position and scale defined on FoodButton.cs. Tapping one toggles it into the persistent FoodData.storedFoods list that survives scene transitions via DontDestroyOnLoad.

● FoodButton.cs ● FoodData singleton ● FridgeScene.cs

3

the pantry cabinet

A wooden cabinet holding the dry pantry essentials: baking soda, baking powder, white and brown sugar, plus the fresh stuff — strawberries, blueberries, kiwi. The CabinetLoader.cs script iterates through FoodData.storedFoods and re-spawns any previously-picked prefabs exactly where they were placed in the fridge scene, so walking between rooms feels continuous.

● CabinetLoader.cs ● Scene 2 ● prefab-driven layout

4

chatting with Mr. Paw

A real-time chat interface where players can ask Mr. Paw general baking questions — proofing times, oven temperatures, technique tips, or just chat for company. ChatManager.cs keeps the full conversation history in a List<ChatMessage>, instantiates a user or AI prefab for each turn, and POSTs to a Google Apps Script proxy that forwards to Gemini. The cat always replies warm and fluffy, with the occasional nya.

● ChatManager.cs ● ScrollRect auto-scroll ● TMP_InputField

5

cozy achievements

When players hit milestones — like making 5 recipes — a hand-drawn pink ribbon slides in from the top of the screen carrying a little gold badge and a cheery description. GlobalAchievements.cs tracks counters, saves unlock state with PlayerPrefs (codes like 12345) so progress persists across sessions, and animates the banner in & out via coroutine.

● GlobalAchievements.cs ● PlayerPrefs ● IEnumerator animations

recipe card · 03

my ingredients in the mix

Baking PawPaw was a small-team project where I wore a few aprons. The whole thing started as a conversation about whether an AI assistant could feel genuinely cozy instead of clinical — and the answer, it turns out, is "yes, if you give it paws."

I led the art direction and visual identity, designed the flow between scenes, and worked on the integration between Unity and the Gemini API via a Google Apps Script endpoint so the game could stay keyless and shippable.

✓

Art direction & illustration

All 17 ingredient sprites, the fridge & cabinet backgrounds, the cat-paw-fish wallpaper, Mr. Paw himself, and every UI frame.

✓

Scene design & game flow

Mapped the main-menu → fridge → cabinet → kitchen → recipe/chat loop across seven Unity scenes.

✓

Character design

Designed Mr. Paw and his expressions — chef hat, whisker curls, a tiny pastry brush in his paw.

✓

Gemini integration

Prompt-engineered the difficulty/time-based recipe generator; wired the Unity client to a GAS proxy.
